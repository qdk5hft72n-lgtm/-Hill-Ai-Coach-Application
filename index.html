Cover Letter

The Workflow
That Changed
Everything

"Describe an AI workflow you built that solved a real problem."

The stuck point was simple: I was trying to pass the NASM Nutrition Coach Certification while working 6-hour valet shifts, with zero mental bandwidth left for active studying by the time I got home.

The solution I built isn't a single tool — it's a learning OS layered across five AI platforms.

Here's how it runs: I dump NASM chapters and lecture materials into NotebookLM, which generates podcast-format audio from my source material. Those episodes run in my earbuds during every shift — passive absorption of certification content while I'm parking cars.

Then I built a pre-quiz protocol in Claude: before every chapter assessment, it generates terminology flashcards targeting my weak spots. NASM-specific terms were my gap, not the underlying concepts. That one move moved my quiz scores.

When nutrition questions needed more depth, I routed them to DeepSeek — better calibrated for evidence-based nutrition specifics than general-purpose models.

The mindset layer runs through a custom Gemini Gem I built called Tony, trained on Tony Robbins' methodology. When I'm running on four hours of sleep and need to open the material anyway, that's the tool that reframes my state first.

I also built an NSS (Nervous System State) self-tracking protocol to know when to push into active study vs. when to stay in passive-only mode. The system gates itself based on readiness.

If I packaged this for others: it lives in Obsidian as a linked vault, with each AI's role documented as a Playbook Card — what to send where, and when.

That's the workflow. Five platforms, one system, zero wasted shift time.

NotebookLM dropped podcast-format audio generation from custom source material. I was on it within 48 hours of the announcement.

What accelerated competence: I didn't test it on generic content. I loaded my actual NASM-CNC chapter notes and had it generate a study session from my own sources. That first real output made the use case click immediately.

Two sessions in, I had a system: one notebook per certification domain, audio generated from chapter summaries and my own flashcard notes, queued on my phone for passive consumption during valet shifts.

The unlock was treating the tool as if it had one specific job — and giving it a real problem to solve on the first attempt.

Pattern I use for every new AI feature:
1Identify the highest-value use case for my actual work
2Test it on a real problem — not a demo prompt
3Document the playbook before I forget what clicked
4Integrate or discard — no half-in tools in the stack
